<td style="color:#000;">
<div id="mp-tfa" style="padding:2px 5px">
<div id="mp-tfa-img" style="float: left; margin: 0.5em 0.9em 0.4em 0em;"><a href="/wiki/File:Chester_A._Arthur_by_Ole_Peter_Hansen_Balling.JPG" class="image" title="Chester Alan Arthur"><img alt="Chester Alan Arthur" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Chester_A._Arthur_by_Ole_Peter_Hansen_Balling.JPG/100px-Chester_A._Arthur_by_Ole_Peter_Hansen_Balling.JPG" width="100" height="118" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Chester_A._Arthur_by_Ole_Peter_Hansen_Balling.JPG/150px-Chester_A._Arthur_by_Ole_Peter_Hansen_Balling.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Chester_A._Arthur_by_Ole_Peter_Hansen_Balling.JPG/200px-Chester_A._Arthur_by_Ole_Peter_Hansen_Balling.JPG 2x" data-file-width="1705" data-file-height="2013"></a></div>
<p><b><a href="/wiki/Chester_A._Arthur" title="Chester A. Arthur">Chester A. Arthur</a></b> (18291886) was the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Presidents_of_the_United_States" title="List of Presidents of the United States">21st</a> <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">President of the United States</a>, from 1881 to 1885. After practicing law in New York City, he served as <a href="/wiki/Quartermaster_general" title="Quartermaster general">quartermaster general</a> in the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Army_National_Guard" title="New York Army National Guard">New York Militia</a> during the <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">American Civil War</a>. Rising quickly in the <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">Republican</a> <a href="/wiki/Political_machine" title="Political machine">political machine</a> run by Senator <a href="/wiki/Roscoe_Conkling" title="Roscoe Conkling">Roscoe Conkling</a>, he was appointed to the lucrative post of <a href="/wiki/Collector_of_the_Port_of_New_York" title="Collector of the Port of New York">Collector of the Port of New York</a> in 1871. In 1878 the new president, <a href="/wiki/Rutherford_B._Hayes" title="Rutherford B. Hayes">Rutherford B. Hayes</a>, fired Arthur as part of a reform measure. When <a href="/wiki/James_Garfield" title="James Garfield" class="mw-redirect">James Garfield</a> won the Republican nomination for president in 1880, Arthur was nominated for <a href="/wiki/Vice_President_of_the_United_States" title="Vice President of the United States">vice president</a> to <a href="/wiki/Ticket_balance" title="Ticket balance">balance the ticket</a>. After <a href="/wiki/Assassination_of_James_Garfield" title="Assassination of James Garfield" class="mw-redirect">Garfield's assassination</a>, Arthur took up the cause of reform, supporting the <a href="/wiki/Pendleton_Civil_Service_Reform_Act" title="Pendleton Civil Service Reform Act">Pendleton Civil Service Reform Act</a>. He presided over the rebirth of the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Navy" title="U.S. Navy" class="mw-redirect">U.S. Navy</a> but was criticized for failing to alleviate a growing federal budget surplus. Suffering from poor health, Arthur retired at the close of his term. Journalist <a href="/wiki/Alexander_McClure" title="Alexander McClure">Alexander McClure</a> later wrote, "No man ever entered the Presidency so profoundly and widely distrusted as Chester Alan Arthur, and no one ever retired&nbsp;... more generally respected, alike by political friend and foe." (<a href="/wiki/Chester_A._Arthur" title="Chester A. Arthur"><b>Full&nbsp;article...</b></a>)</p>

<p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award_for_Best_Country_Collaboration_with_Vocals" title="Grammy Award for Best Country Collaboration with Vocals">Grammy Award for Best Country Collaboration with Vocals</a></b> was an honor presented at the <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Awards</a>, a ceremony that was established in 1958 and originally called the Gramophone Awards, to quality <a href="/wiki/Country_music" title="Country music">country music</a> collaborations for artists who do not normally perform together. Honors in several categories are presented at the ceremony annually by the <a href="/wiki/National_Academy_of_Recording_Arts_and_Sciences" title="National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences">National Academy of Recording Arts and Sciences</a> of the United States to "honor artistic achievement, technical proficiency and overall excellence in the recording industry, without regard to album sales or chart position". Originally called the Best Country Vocal Performance, Duet, the award was first presented to <a href="/wiki/Kenny_Rogers" title="Kenny Rogers">Kenny Rogers</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ronnie_Milsap" title="Ronnie Milsap">Ronnie Milsap</a> at the <a href="/wiki/30th_Grammy_Awards" title="30th Grammy Awards" class="mw-redirect">30th Grammy Awards</a> in 1988 for the single "<a href="/wiki/Make_No_Mistake,_She%27s_Mine" title="Make No Mistake, She's Mine" class="mw-redirect">Make No Mistake, She's Mine</a>". <a href="/wiki/Alison_Krauss" title="Alison Krauss">Alison Krauss</a> <i>(pictured)</i> holds the record for having the most wins in this category, with a total of five. She is followed by seven others, who have all won the award twice. Among the most nominated are <a href="/wiki/Emmylou_Harris" title="Emmylou Harris">Emmylou Harris</a> and <a href="/wiki/Willie_Nelson" title="Willie Nelson">Willie Nelson</a>, both nine-time nominees. <p>The <b><a href="/wiki/Jefferson_Memorial" title="Jefferson Memorial">Jefferson Memorial</a></b> is a <a href="/wiki/United_States_presidential_memorial" title="United States presidential memorial" class="mw-redirect">presidential memorial</a> in <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a> designed by the architect <a href="/wiki/John_Russell_Pope" title="John Russell Pope">John Russell Pope</a> and built by the <a href="/wiki/Philadelphia" title="Philadelphia">Philadelphia</a> contractor <a href="/wiki/John_McShain" title="John McShain">John McShain</a> from 1939 to 1943.
